Introduction

Our App, UniFresh, aims to help RMIT staff and students find healthy eating options on campus, providing a simple and intuitive layout to make choosing and ordering a healthy option easy and fast to cater to the busy lifestyles of our target audience.

Design Principles

Our App opens with a map screen, marking all the local healthy eateries on campus. You can tap the green markers to open the info screen for each eatery and see its reviews, menu and health rating.

We also utilise bottom bar navigation for easy access to the application’s features for users so they don’t need to continuously go back to a main menu to access the multiple features of the app. It also helps new users discover more functionality of the app by having it more accessible more often.

Aesthetic and Minimalist Design, Dynamic Layout: there are minimal buttons on the home screen of the app; menu items are hidden until required.

We have a Profile page accessible from the Profile button on the Navigation Bar. This Profile page is a convenient spot for the Users to view and edit their profile information including Payment Options, Location Settings and their Favourite Restaurants.

Recognition Rather than Recall, Consistency and standards: The icons  in the bottom bar use familiar and recognisable icons for the Profile, Search, Settings and Food buttons that the user may be familiar with from other apps, the profile design is similar with the profile layout of many mobile apps to create this familiarity and help the user navigate.

We have a built in calorie tracker with customizable calorie limiting and a cheat day function, with a visual representation of the day’s calorie consumption displayed prominently as suggested by 95% of survey respondents. This allows the user freedom to choose the caloric restrictions they want and it will help them keep track of how they’re eating. There are also preset recommended meal plans to help users who may not be as experienced at planning their calorie intake, as also requested by users in the survey.

Colour and opacity: the calorie tracker changes colour from green to red as the user approaches their calorie intake for the day, using the connotations of these colours to warn the user. Additionally, when dialogue boxes are open as seen in the screenshot, the rest of the screen is darkened to tell the user that it is not active.

When using the app you can view the eating locations around you via map-view or list-view, either way once you have selected the location it will open the product page. There’s an easy out button in the top right if you change your mind about the place you want to eat.


We have easy access on this screen to some functional buttons to Order Now (which lets you view the menu options as well as order food for pickup), Navigate to the Restaurant and a Favourites Button if you want to order from this place again.

We have a dual rating system that lets users rate not only based on taste and experience but also we provide a health rating for each location to help users at a glance find out how healthy a restaurant is.

Consistency and Standards: A 5 Star rating system is standard across most food apps and review systems. Our health rating system (displayed alongside the traditional rating) is also out of 5 to be consistent with what users will recognise and understand.

You can use the Navigation button in the screen pictured above to access Guided Walking Navigation to the chosen restaurant to assist the user in finding their way to the restaurant.

If you have placed a current order in the app and are on your way to collect it there will be an additional bubble on screen to view the order at any time.

User Control and Freedom: The user can see that they have a current order in the system and can easily navigate to that order from this menu as well as stop the navigation and view the information of their destination at any time. It is important to make sure the user can easily switch between screens so they feel in control and not trapped in any one screen.

Error prevention: the clickable areas for all the buttons in the app are oversized in order to facilitate user input and alleviate inaccurate taps.

Here is the current orders screen, you can exit back to the map via the return button and easily see your basic order details from this screen including your calorie intake from the food, how much it cost and your order reference number for when you get to the restaurant. This screen is only accessible after you have placed an order but before you have collected it.

Prominence, aesthetic & minimalistic design - although it also serves to give the user details about their order, this screen is designed to be shown to the cashier at the establishment upon arrival so they know the right order for the customer. As such, the order number is in bold, which makes it more prominent than the other elements on the page except the page title. The restaurant name is the next heaviest font weight in case the user ordered from multiple restaurants. The other details about the order that are mainly for the user’s benefit are displayed in grey to make them less prominent and prevent clutter.

This screen shows right after you have confirmed your order, post making payment if you chose to pre-pay for your order.

Once the order is placed the app automatically adds the calories from your order onto your calorie tracker for the day.

After you have placed your order you can quickly view all your active orders to show when you get to the restaurant, enable navigation on the map to that restaurant or view your daily meal tracker.

Flexibility and Efficiency of Use, Error Prevention: Food items the user has ordered will automatically add to the total calories for the day without the user having to input. This is an easy way to prevent errors tracking their eating for the user, the user is notified of the calories being added by a graphic on screen with the recently added items underneath so they are aware of what has been added.

An interesting function we decided to add in is a cheat day function. The cheat day automatically activates if you exceed your calorie limit by a certain amount, or can be activated manually. It will keep track of how many days you go without a cheat day, gamifying the diet experience to incentivise healthier habits. You can also resume your diet if you manually activated the cheat day.

User Control and Freedom: The user can manually choose to enter or exit the cheat day function so they are not always restricted to eating within their profile designated limits. They are not locked into a cheat day once they have entered it.
